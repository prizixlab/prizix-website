# PRIZIX INC. Website Blueprint

## 1. Visual & Aesthetic Direction

**Core Identity:**
PRIZIX is a commerce systems studio founded in 2004. The focus is on brand-first, systems-driven architecture. There is no founder spotlight, no individual names, no marketing hype, and no "startup" aesthetics. The tone is welcoming but authoritative, accessible yet not entry-level.

**Target Audience:**
Shopify brands serious about scaling, prepared to invest in structured infrastructure, AI visibility, automation, and revenue optimization.

**Visual Language:**
- **Base:** Deep charcoal
- **Primary Accent:** Electric blue
- **Secondary Accent:** Subtle violet
- **Layout:** Strict grid layout 
- **Typography:** Strong hierarchy (e.g., modern grotesque or geometric sans-serif fonts like Inter or Roboto, enforcing a professional, engineered look)
- **Motion:** Minimal, intentional motion
- **Overall Feel:** Professional, engineered, structured
- **Avoid:** Neon gradients, AI blob visuals, marketing hype styling

---

## 2. SEO & AI-Search Structure Guidelines

Every page must adhere to the following technical and semantic structure to be "AI-searchable":
- **Title Tag & Meta Description:** Unique and descriptive per page.
- **Headings:** Single, strict `<h1>` per page. Clean semantic hierarchy (`<h2>`, `<h3>`, etc.).
- **Internal Linking:** Strategic semantic linking between system pillars and services.
- **Schemas:** 
  - FAQ schema blocks where applicable.
  - JSON-LD placeholders for `Organization`, `Service`, `WebPage`, and `Article` entities.
- **Layout:** Machine-readable, structured, and parseable by LLMs.

## 4. Final Experience Check

Upon reviewing the wireframes and final copy, a scaling founder should feel:
> *"This team builds real systems that prepare our store for the AI-driven future."*

It must explicitly *not* feel like:
> *"This is another Shopify agency."* 
*(Achieved by removing subjective adjectives, removing hype, emphasizing systems over campaigns, and maintaining strict architectural aesthetics).*
